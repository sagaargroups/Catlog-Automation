Catalog Automation App: A Fun Helper for Your Spreadsheets!

Do you ever play with toy blocks and wish you could build many similar things really fast without repeating the same steps? Or you have a list of favorite candies and want to make many lists with slightly different candies in each?

That’s what this Catalog Automation App does with Google Sheets™. It acts like a super‑smart robot helper that fills out your lists quickly and consistently.

What Our Robot Helper Does (The Big Picture)

  • Imagine you have headers like “Color”, “Size”, “Type”. You want rows such as:
    • Red, Small, Car
    • Blue, Medium, Ball
    • Green, Big, Doll
  • Tell the robot what to write; it writes it for you.
  • It can remember your setups so you can reuse them.
  • It can write many rows at once using smart rules.

The Robot’s Brain (Code.js) — What it Thinks About

getAvailableRanges(): Finding Treasure Spots

  • What it does: Finds named ranges in your sheet to discover useful lists.
  • Why it’s important: Helps the robot locate options to pick from.

getValuesFromActiveRange(headerName): Reading What You Point To

  • What it does: Reads values from the current selection (active range) you highlight.
  • Why it’s important: Lets you add fresh options to dropdowns from your sheet.

updateAutomationOptions(updatedAutomationData): Changing the Robot’s Memories

  • What it does: Updates a saved automation in the robot’s memory sheet.
  • Why it’s important: Improve saved helpers without starting over.

DB_SHEET_ID and DB_SHEET_NAME: The Robot’s Secret Diary

  • What it does: Identifies the separate memory sheet (ID and name).
  • Why it’s important: Stores your saved automations safely.

_addNumberingToOptions(options): Giving Numbers to Your Choices

  • What it does: Turns ["Apple", "Banana"] into ["1. Apple", "2. Banana", "0. BLANK"].
  • Why it’s important: Makes choices tidy and easy to reference.

_removeNumberingFromOption(numberedOption): Taking Numbers Off

  • What it does: Converts “1. Apple” back to “Apple”.
  • Why it’s important: Writes clean values into the sheet.

onOpen(): Saying “Hello!” When You Open the Book

  • What it does: Adds the “Catalog Automation” menu.
  • Why it’s important: One‑click access to the robot helper.

_getColumnLetterFromIndex(index): Giving Names to Columns

  • What it does: Converts zero‑based indices into A, B, C…
  • Why it’s important: Human‑friendly column labels.

openSidebar(): Opening the Robot’s Playroom

  • What it does: Opens the app’s sidebar UI in Google Sheets™.
  • Why it’s important: Where you give the robot instructions.

getHeadersAndOptions(): Learning About Your List

  • What it does: Reads header row and existing validation options.
  • Why it’s important: Understands your sheet’s structure and choices.

writeRow(selectedValues): Writing One Line Quickly

  • What it does: Appends a single row with your chosen values.
  • Why it’s important: Fast, one‑off additions.

writeMultipleRows(automationData): Writing Many Lines with Smart Rules

  • What it does: Writes multiple rows following rules (repeat, advance options, fix values).
  • Why it’s important: Huge time saver for similar rows.

saveAutomation(name, automationData, userEmail): Remembering Your Best Ideas

  • What it does: Saves named automations with owner email.
  • Why it’s important: Reuse and share safely.

listAutomations(userEmail): Showing Your Saved Ideas

  • What it does: Lists automations saved by the active user.
  • Why it’s important: Quickly load your favorites.

addNumberingToOptionsServer(options): Robot Adding Numbers for the Playroom

  • What it does: Prepares numbered options for the sidebar UI.
  • Why it’s important: Consistent, clear display in the sidebar.

getActiveUserEmail(): Who Are You?

  • What it does: Gets the active user’s email via Google™ account context.
  • Why it’s important: Keeps automations user‑scoped.

The Robot’s Playroom (Sidebar.html) — What You See and Do

<h2>Catalog Automation</h2>

  • What it is: The app title in the sidebar.
  • Why it’s important: Clear entry point.

Saved Automations

  • What it is: List of your saved setups.
  • Why it’s important: Load and reuse fast.

Automation Fields

  • What it is: Drag‑and‑drop headers into containers, pick options, build logic.
  • Why it’s important: Create smart, organized inputs.
  • + Create New Automation: Start fresh.
  • + Add Container: Group related fields (e.g., “Toy Details”).

Fields & Containers

  • What they are: Each field maps to a header; containers hold fields. Dropdowns let you pick values.
  • Why they’re important: Visual organization, easy option management.

+ Button Next to Dropdowns

  • What it is: When clicked, imports values from your active range selection.
  • Why it’s important: Quickly enrich dropdown options from the sheet.

Rules Against Container

Rule 1. Multiply in number

  • What it is: Repeat a container’s values N times.
  • Why it’s important: Batch‑create identical segments.

Rule 2. Which option to start from

  • What it is: Start at a specific numbered option, then advance per row.
  • Why it’s important: Vary choices across rows (e.g., Banana, Cherry, …).

Rule 3. All Time Fix Container

  • What it is: Lock a container’s picks regardless of other rules.
  • Why it’s important: Keep critical fields constant.

Action Buttons

  • Fire (Write Data): Execute writing to the sheet based on rules.
  • Update Automation: Save edits to a loaded automation.
  • Save as Automation: Name and save a new automation.

How It All Works Together (Like a Team!)

  1. You open your Google Sheets™ file (onOpen() adds the menu).
  2. You click “Catalog Automation” (openSidebar() opens the sidebar).
  3. The robot inspects your headers and options (getHeadersAndOptions()).
  4. You build an automation: drag fields, pick options, add rules.
  5. You click “Fire (Write Data)” or “Save as Automation”.
  6. The robot writes new rows (sheet.appendRow()) automatically.
